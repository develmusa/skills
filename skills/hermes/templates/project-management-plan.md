### Aim and purpose of the document

The initial development of the project management plan is influenced by the decision on next steps in the initiation phase. The decision on the approach in particular -traditional or agile - influences the execution of the tasks and the structure and content of the outcomes.

The project management plan contains the overall planning of the project and the essential regulations on methods, techniques, roles, and tools determined for the specific project. The project management plan serves as a uniform basis for action for everyone involved in the project. As part of project organization, the project management plan ensures that the responsibilities and the division of tasks between service procurers, internal service providers, and external suppliers where applicable are sufficiently clearly regulated and documented. As the project progresses, it is continuously fleshed out and updated according to the principle of rolling planning and steering.

In agile solution development, the schedule for the execution phase is combined with the release plan. This release plan specifies when operation is activated for each release. It also specifies whether the (optional) decision on release is mandatory in the project or not.

At the end of the phase, the project management plan is adapted to the changed conditions for the next phase. Before the closure phase is released, the project management plan for project closure is also prepared and adapted accordingly.

## Quality assurance

Testing is set out in the test concept. Reviews are set out in the review plan chapter of the project management plan. The following review methods are used in the project.

| Review method | Description |
| --- | --- |
| ... |   |
| Written review (WR) | The outcome is sent to the reviewers by email. They check the outcome independently of each other and prepare a review report with the findings. The findings are corrected by the author or rejected with written justification, which is sent to the reviewer by email. |
| Verbal review (VR) | The written findings are discussed in a review meeting during which the necessary adjustments/measures are decided, assigned to the people responsible and scheduled. |
| Walk-through (WT) | A walk-through is a less formal examination than a review. By going through the outcomes available at a certain point in time, the examiner tries to get a picture of the current state of progress. |
| ... | ... |

If outcomes are accepted by means of a consultation of specialist representatives, this review is carried out with a written review.

## Risk management

The project risks are first listed in the execution order and then, updated in each case, continued in the project status report and phase report.

To assess the risks, each risk must be understood in relation to its likelihood of occurrence and level of impact. The variables for this are specified here.

The assessment scales below can be adopted or adapted to the risk management scale of the core organization.

Rating scale for probability of occurrence
> _Adapt to your own needs_

| Evaluation | Description of probability of occurrence |
| --- | --- |
| 1 = low | Improbable, or less than 20% |
| 2 = medium | moderately probable, or 20-50% |
| 3 = high | highly probable, or more than 50% |

Rating scale for level of impact
> _Adapt to your own needs_

| Evaluation | Impact on project outcome | Impact on deadline | Impact on costs |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 = low | Minor deficiencies | Up to 1 month, or minor | None |
| 2 = medium | Significant deficiencies | 1-3 months, or significant | 5%-20%, or significant |
| 3 = high | Serious deficiencies | More than 3 months, or serious | More than 20%, or serious |

The coordinates for mapping the risk in the matrix are then derived from these values. The arrow (e.g. risk 2) can be used to show the change relative to the last assessment.

## Escalation procedure

Escalation is a special measure that should be taken with caution and, if possible, only in exceptional cases. It is always desirable and beneficial in the context of a project to first try all other measures of targeted conflict resolution and to bring all of one's own natural ability and communication skills to bear, as one escalation often leads to another.

The procedure in the event of conflict escalation is defined once and applies for the entire duration of the project. However, the procedure can be adapted at any time.

Within the framework of a project, escalation occurs because of an unresolved conflict or problem. Problems that cannot be solved by project management due to a lack of powers or sufficient means are escalated to project steering. Project steering treats these problems with the necessary priority and urgency.

The escalation process is described in a neutral and role-specific way. It defines the gradual delegation of an unending conflict, or problem that cannot be solved, to a higher project level: from the management level, to the steering level and then further within the steering level to the next level.

The procedure described is intended, among other things, to define the circumstances under which any hierarchy levels may be skipped and when this is not appropriate.

Documents with time reference

Documents with a clear time reference contain the date in the file name.

As it is customary that documents can always be sorted by date, practice has shown that putting the document name first is more beneficial (document name YYMMDD.xxx) than vice versa. Otherwise, it is not possible to sort in alphabetical order.
