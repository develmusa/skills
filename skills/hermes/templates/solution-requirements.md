## Introduction

The solution requirements include all the requirements that the future system or product must fulfill. This includes the specialist and technical requirements, but also expected characteristics, functions, legal and solution-relevant and regulatory specifications, operational, support and security requirements, as well as requirements for optimization and defect correction of any existing products or systems.

In traditional solution development, the solution requirements are worked out in final detail on the basis of the study and situation analysis and, if necessary, successively updated during solution development via change management.

In agile solution development, the solution requirements are first compiled as an "initial product backlog" in the execution phase on the basis of the study and the situation analysis. As part of self-organized agile execution, changes are documented in the change status list and are only updated in the solution requirements via a change request if the project objectives, the budget or the time frame of the overall project change (see manage changes task).
